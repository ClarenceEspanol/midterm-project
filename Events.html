Activities (February 21-23, 2024)

A. Parade

A parade is a festive gathering where individuals, often representing various groups or organizations, come together to march or travel through public streets or designated routes. This collective display typically occurs in celebration of a significant event, such as a holiday, cultural festival, historical milestone, or achievement. Participants in a parade may include marching bands, dancers, performers, costumed characters, community organizations, military units, and floats adorned with decorations relevant to the theme of the occasion. Spectators line the streets, sidewalks, or designated viewing areas to watch the procession, enjoying the lively atmosphere, colorful displays, music, and entertainment provided by the participants. Parades serve as a means of uniting communities, fostering a sense of pride, solidarity, and shared identity, while also providing an opportunity for people to come together and celebrate common values, traditions, and achievements.

B. MASS

The Mass is the central act of worship in the Catholic Church and some other Christian denominations. During the Mass, bread and wine are believed to be transformed into the body and blood of Jesus Christ through transubstantiation. This transformation occurs during the consecration by the priest. The Mass includes prayers, readings from the Bible, a homily, and the celebration of the Eucharist. It commemorates the Last Supper of Jesus with his disciples and serves as a means of grace, strengthening believers' faith and uniting them with Christ and one another.

C. Opening Program(Banner Raising & Bleachers Yell)

Banner raising ceremonies mark the start of a sports season, where a team's emblem is raised in the venue, symbolizing unity and pride. It displays the team's name, logo, and achievements, boosting morale and setting a positive tone. Bleacher yell competitions, however, involve sections of the crowd competing in spirited chants and cheers for their team. Divided by class or groups, participants showcase their enthusiasm with synchronized movements and props. Judges or the crowd decide the winner based on volume, creativity, and teamwork. Both traditions foster camaraderie, school spirit, and community engagement, enhancing the overall fan experience during sporting events.

D. Laro ng Lahi

"Laro ng Lahi" translates to "Traditional Games" in English. It refers to a variety of indigenous or traditional games that are played in different cultures and regions. These games often have deep cultural significance and are passed down through generations. They typically involve physical activities, teamwork, strategy, and sometimes even storytelling. "Laro ng Lahi" promotes cultural heritage preservation, physical fitness, social interaction, and often serves as a source of entertainment and community bonding. It's a way for people to connect with their roots, learn about their cultural heritage, and celebrate their identity through play.

E. MR. and MS. OLLCF

A Mr. and Ms. pageant is a competition where individuals typically compete for titles such as Mr. or Ms. OLLCF, representing a certain organization, community, or even a country. Participants showcase various attributes such as personality, intelligence, talent, and physical appearance through different segments like talent performances, interviews, and evening wear presentations. Judges evaluate contestants based on predetermined criteria, and winners are often chosen based on their overall presentation, charisma, and ability to represent the values of the organization or community they're representing. These pageants serve as platforms for personal growth, community involvement, and sometimes scholarship opportunities.
